\section{About Me}
I am a second-year Ph.D. student at the \href{https://www.eecs.psu.edu/departments/EECS-Departments-Computer-Science-Engineering3.aspx}{Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE)} of \href{https://www.psu.edu/}{Pennsylvania State University}, advised by \href{https://pike.psu.edu/dongwon/}{Prof.\ Dongwon Lee} and co-advised by \href{https://www.cse.psu.edu/~kxc104/}{Prof.\ Kyusun Choi}. Previously, I obtained my bachelor's and master's degrees from the \href{https://cse.buet.ac.bd/}{Department of CSE}, \href{https://www.buet.ac.bd/web/#/}{Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET)}, where I also served as a \href{https://cse.buet.ac.bd/faculty/faculty_detail/toufikuzzaman}{Lecturer and Assistant Professor}.

My research focuses on \textbf{trustworthy and reliable AI}, with a particular interest in understanding how knowledge and facts are stored, retrieved, and manipulated within Large Language Models (LLMs)---spanning mechanistic interpretability, knowledge editing, and LLM unlearning. I also work on applying \textbf{agentic AI systems for red-team testing automation}, including autonomous agents for web application security testing.

I am a co-leader of \textbf{Team Lion-0xA}, one of 10 global finalists in the \href{https://www.psu.edu/news/research/story/ist-engineering-students-competing-global-amazon-nova-ai-challenge}{Amazon Nova AI Challenge 2026}, supported by a quarter-million-dollar Amazon grant. Our team's submission, \textit{``Autonomous Co-Design and Multi-Agent Red Teaming for Advancing Trusted Agentic AI,''} was featured in \href{https://www.psu.edu/news/research/story/ist-engineering-students-competing-global-amazon-nova-ai-challenge}{Penn State News}.
